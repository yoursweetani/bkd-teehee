Why This Data is Collected
Reports and any submitted evidence are collected solely to support disciplinary action against prohibited acts on school grounds — including but not limited to tobacco and vaping use, substance use, bullying, vandalism, and other violations of school policy.
What Data is Collected
A description of the incident, its location, the approximate date and time, and photo evidence of the student(s) being accused. No information about the reporter is ever collected — all submissions are entirely and permanently anonymous.
Who Can Access This Data
Submitted reports and evidence are accessible only to school administrators and school-authorized student managers who have been officially designated to handle disciplinary matters.
How Long Data is Kept
All submitted data is deleted immediately after the verification process is completed, regardless of whether the report is confirmed or dismissed.
What Verification Involves
① Incident Verification — Confirming whether the accused student did in fact commit a prohibited act.

② Identity Matching — Verifying the student identified matches the individual in any submitted evidence.

③ Evidence Integrity Check — Reviewing media for tampering: AI-generated images, digital manipulation (e.g. Photoshop), or content taken out of context.
⚠️
Warning: False Reporting is a Punishable Offense
Deliberately submitting a false report, fabricating or tampering with evidence, or reporting a student out of personal malice — even anonymously — is a serious violation of school policy. Students found responsible will face disciplinary consequences, including suspension.
🛡️This report is fully anonymous. No information about you is collected, stored, or traceable.
